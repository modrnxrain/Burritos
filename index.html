The name burrito as applied to the dish possibly derives from the appearance of bedrolls and packs that donkeys carried.[4] The word "burrito" appears in the Dictionary of Mexicanisms from 1895. It is the term used in the regional area of Guanajuato to describe a "Tortilla rolled, with meat or other food within, that in Yucatan is known as coçito, and Cuernavaca and Mexico, as taco."[5] Having been published in 1895, this fact discredits the etymology that notes the origin of the "burrito" during the Mexican Revolution (1910-1921) in the City of Cuidad Juarez by Juan Méndez. In other regions of Mexico such as in the state of Tamaulipas, similar types of food are known as "flauta" (flute).
